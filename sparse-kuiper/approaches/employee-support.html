Partnerships Pillar

Employee Support Systems

Helping organisations understand how peer support and internal wellbeing structures can create healthier workplaces — without needing expensive external programmes.

← Back to Our Work

Not every organisation can afford an Employee Assistance Programme. But every organisation can create simple internal support structures that help people feel seen, heard, and supported — before problems escalate into crises.

Workplace Wellness Africa raises awareness about peer support models and helps leaders understand how to create first-response capabilities within their teams using their existing people.

Ideas That Work

01

Peer Support Circles

Small groups of colleagues who meet regularly to check in on each other. No clinical training required — just a structured space for honest conversation about how people are actually doing. The evidence shows that feeling heard by peers is one of the strongest protective factors against burnout.

02

Mental Health Champions

Nominating and training a small number of employees to be visible points of contact for wellbeing. Not therapists — just people who know the basics, can listen without judgement, and know where to refer someone when the situation is beyond their scope. Through our partner trainers, we can help set this up.

03

Safe Spaces

A physical quiet space in the office — not a meeting room, just somewhere an overwhelmed employee can retreat for 15 minutes of quiet. No questions asked. This costs nothing to set up and can make a significant difference in high-pressure environments.
