Advocacy Pillar

Inclusive Workplaces

Raising awareness about neurodiversity and helping leaders understand how to create environments where all cognitive styles can contribute.

← Back to Our Work

ADHD, Autism, Dyslexia, and other cognitive variations are far more common than most workplaces realise. In Kenya, there is very little awareness of neurodiversity in professional settings — meaning talented people are excluded or forced to mask who they are just to keep a job.

Workplace Wellness Africa raises awareness among leaders about neurodiversity and helps them understand simple, practical ways to make workplaces more accommodating for different cognitive styles.

What We Advocate For

Understanding Neurodiversity

Most managers have never heard the term "neurodiversity." We raise awareness about what it means, how common it is, and why it matters — helping leaders move from confusion or bias to understanding and accommodation.

Flexible Work Structures

Open-plan offices, constant meetings, and rigid schedules can be particularly challenging for neurodivergent employees. We help leaders understand that small environmental changes — quiet spaces, flexible hours, written instructions — can make a significant difference without being costly or disruptive.

Reducing Noise, Increasing Focus

The "always-on" expectation punishes people who need focused blocks of time. We advocate for simple practices like moving status updates to written memos and reserving meetings for actual decision-making — changes that benefit everyone, not just neurodivergent employees.

Making Space for Every Mind

Interested in learning more about how your workplace can be more inclusive? We're happy to have a conversation about what practical steps might work for your organisation.
